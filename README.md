# Lyapunov-Swarm-API

## 🌐 Vision 
This project focuses on decentralized control for large-scale autonomous systems, aiming to revolutionize **Urban Air Mobility (UAM)** and **Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS)**. 
The core objective is to achieve **robustness by design** under non-ideal communication environments (Shannon-limited bandwidth) to enable safe, scalable multi-agent coordination in **emergency response and public infrastructure**.

## 🚀 Why This Matters 
As we transition toward the era of embodied AI and autonomous traffic networks, centralized cloud control faces physical bottlenecks:
1. **Communication Latency**: Shannon’s Theorem limits real-time status updates in dense clusters.
2. **Robustness**: Traditional RL-based policies often behave like "black boxes" in edge cases. 
3. **Safety & Efficiency**: We aim to integrate decentralized verification to ensure operational reliability in shared autonomous networks.

## 🛠️ The Core Research Problem 
In large-scale public transportation and smart logistics, centralized systems face single-point-of-failure risks. My research focuses on:
- **Robustness in UAM**: Ensuring safe coordination of autonomous delivery swarms in complex urban corridors.
- **Traffic Efficiency**: Optimizing multi-agent traffic flow (V2X) to reduce urban carbon footprint and congestion using Lyapunov-based stability certification.
- **Data Integrity**: Using decentralized ledgers to ensure transparent and tamper-proof operational data in shared autonomous networks.

## 📂 Current Progress 
- [ ] **Phase 1: Math Foundation**: Analyzing Lyapunov energy dissipation in non-linear systems to ensure stability.
- [ ] **Phase 2: Simulation Environment**: Constructing a multi-agent sandbox using Python/ROS2 to test swarm behavior.
- [ ] **Phase 3: Policy Optimization**: Integrating stability certification into MARL (Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning) models.

## 💡 Engineering Philosophy 
"I believe the future of AI is not just about intelligence, but about **robust physical control**. As a student of the future, I aim to bridge the gap between classical control theory and modern AI to build reliable autonomous systems for public benefit."
